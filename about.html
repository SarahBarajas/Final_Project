What is Autism?

Autism, or autism spectrum disorder (ASD), is a developmental
disorder that affects how individuals communicate, interact,
and behave. The term “spectrum” reflects the wide range of symptoms and
severity levels that can occur. People with autism often have difficulties with social communication and may engage
in repetitive behaviors or have highly focused interests. These symptoms typically appear in early childhood,
although the exact age of onset can vary

The causes of autism are not fully understood, but it is believed to result from a combination
of genetic and environmental factors. There is no single known cause,
and it is not caused by parenting or any specific actions by parents. Early diagnosis and
intervention are crucial as they can significantly improve the quality of life for individuals with autism.
Interventions often include behavioral therapies, educational support,
and sometimes medications to manage specific symptoms

Living with autism can present unique challenges, but it also comes with distinct strengths.
Many individuals with autism have exceptional abilities in areas such as mathematics, music, or art.
Understanding and acceptance from society are essential to support individuals with autism in reaching
their full potential. Each person with autism is unique, and recognizing their individual strengths
and needs is key to providing effective support and fostering an inclusive environment
